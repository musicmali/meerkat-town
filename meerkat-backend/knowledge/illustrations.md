# Illustration Fundamentals

## Types of Illustration

### Traditional Illustration
- Pencil drawing: Graphite, charcoal, colored pencils
- Ink illustration: Pen and ink, brush and ink
- Watercolor: Transparent, fluid medium
- Acrylic/Oil painting: Opaque, rich colors
- Gouache: Opaque watercolor

### Digital Illustration
- Vector illustration: Scalable, clean lines (Illustrator, Affinity Designer)
- Raster illustration: Painterly, textured (Photoshop, Procreate)
- 3D illustration: Dimensional, rendered (Blender, Cinema 4D)
- Pixel art: Retro, grid-based

### Illustration Styles
- Flat design: Simple shapes, minimal shadows
- Line art: Clean outlines, minimal fill
- Isometric: 3D objects at consistent angle
- Hand-drawn: Organic, imperfect lines
- Minimalist: Essential elements only
- Maximalist: Detailed, busy compositions
- Realistic: Lifelike representation
- Abstract: Non-representational shapes
- Cartoon: Exaggerated, playful
- Editorial: Conceptual, storytelling

## Core Skills

### Drawing Fundamentals
- Line: Weight, quality, direction
- Shape: Geometric and organic forms
- Form: 3D representation through shading
- Value: Light and dark gradients
- Texture: Surface quality
- Space: Depth and perspective

### Perspective
- One-point: Single vanishing point (corridors, roads)
- Two-point: Two vanishing points (building corners)
- Three-point: Three points (looking up/down at buildings)
- Atmospheric: Objects fade with distance

### Composition
- Rule of thirds: Divide canvas into 9 equal parts
- Golden ratio: 1:1.618 proportions
- Leading lines: Guide viewer's eye
- Framing: Use elements to frame subject
- Focal point: Clear center of interest
- Negative space: Strategic empty areas

### Color in Illustration
- Limited palette: 3-5 colors for cohesion
- Color temperature: Warm vs cool tones
- Saturation: Intensity of colors
- Value contrast: Light vs dark for depth
- Color harmony: Using color wheel relationships

## Character Design

### Anatomy Basics
- Proportions: Adult ~7-8 heads tall
- Gesture: Line of action, flow
- Silhouette: Recognizable shape
- Expression: Face and body language

### Character Elements
- Shape language: Round (friendly), angular (aggressive), square (stable)
- Distinctive features: Memorable characteristics
- Color palette: Character-specific colors
- Costume design: Reflects personality/role
- Accessories: Props that define character

### Character Turnarounds
- Front view
- Side view (profile)
- Back view
- 3/4 view
- Expression sheet
- Action poses

## Illustration Workflow

### Research & Concept
1. Understand the brief/goal
2. Gather references
3. Brainstorm ideas
4. Create mood boards

### Sketching Phase
1. Thumbnail sketches (small, quick ideas)
2. Rough sketch (composition)
3. Refined sketch (details)
4. Client/review feedback

### Final Artwork
1. Clean line art
2. Base colors (flats)
3. Shading and highlights
4. Details and textures
5. Final adjustments

## Digital Illustration Tools

### Software
- Procreate: iPad, intuitive, natural brushes

- Clip Studio Paint: Comics and manga focused

- Affinity Designer: Vector, one-time purchase
- Krita: Free, open source, painting focused

### Hardware
- Drawing tablets: Wacom, Huion, XP-Pen
- Pen displays: Wacom Cintiq, Huion Kamvas
- iPad Pro + Apple Pencil
- Surface Pro with pen

### Brush Types
- Hard round: Clean edges, line art
- Soft round: Blending, gradients
- Textured: Natural media feel
- Custom brushes: Specialized effects
